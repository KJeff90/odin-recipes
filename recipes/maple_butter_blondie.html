Why Applebees Will Always Have a Special Place In My Heart

Okay now look, first of all, I'm completely aware that Applebees is not exactly a five star dining establishment. And I'm not here to try to convince you otherwise; HOWEVER, real talk, have you ever HAD one of their Maple Butter blondies? Because BAYBEE, idk what it is about it, but them thangs just hit different. Whether it's the specific recipe for the blondie itself, the manner in which they bake it, or the 372 metric tons of sugar that go into making the maple butter sauce, there's just something about them that has earned Applebees a spot in my hall of fame for no other reason than to go back and have a blondie every once in a while. But you know the only thing more satisfying than having one of their blondies? Making one yourself. And today, I'll be sharing with you the secret of how to do just that from the comfort of your owbn home.

Ingredients

For the Blondies

    Melted Butter
    Brown Sugar
    Large Egg
    Vanilla Extract
    Salt
    All Purpose Flour
    Butterscotch Chips

For the Maple Butter Sauce

    Unsalted Butter
    Sugar
    Cream Cheese (softened)
    Real Maple Syrup
    Brown Sugar
    Ice Cream (Butter Pecan is my personal preference, but plain or french Vanilla work as well)

Steps

Blondies

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees F. Grease an 8x8-inch baking dish. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, combine butter and brown sugar. Mix until smooth.
  3. Add egg, vanilla, and salt. Stir until blended.
  4. Mix in flour until combined. Fold in butterscotch chips.
  5. Spread the batter into the baking dish. Bake for 25 minutes.

Maple Butter Sauce

  1. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine all maple butter sauce ingredients.
  2. Stir until smooth. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring frequently.
  3. Once the blondies are cooked and cooled completely, cut blondies into squares, plate with ice cream, sprinkle with chopped pecans, and drizzle sauce over the top.
